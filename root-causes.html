Root Causes

Explore the social, economic, and systemic factors that drive criminal activity and hinder peace and justice in the country.

Social Inequality: A Major Driver

Income Inequality

Social Inequality is a significant contributor to crime in Brazil. The vast disparity between rich and poor creates a fertile ground for criminal activities.

Key data point: The richest 1% of Brazilians own 28.3% of the country's wealth.

This stark imbalance leads to limited opportunities for many, pushing some towards illegal means of survival.
