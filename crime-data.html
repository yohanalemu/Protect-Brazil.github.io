Crime Data in Brazil

Homicide Rates in Brazil

Brazil has one of the highest homicide rates globally, with over 40,000 murders reported annually in recent years. These alarming numbers disproportionately affect young men and communities in lower socioeconomic areas, creating a cycle of violence that hinders progress.

The chart below highlights trends in homicide rates over the past decade, showing regional variations and periods of heightened violence. Understanding these patterns is crucial for targeted interventions.

Deaths Caused by Police Intervention

Deaths resulting from police actions represent a significant portion of violent incidents in Brazil. Reports indicate that excessive use of force is a critical issue, particularly in urban areas. These deaths have sparked debates about accountability, systemic corruption, and the urgent need for police reform.

Below is a visualization of the number of deaths caused by police intervention, highlighting both regional disparities and concerning trends over time.

Impact on Demographics

Violence and systemic issues impact specific groups more acutely. For example:

  • Young men aged 15-29 represent the majority of homicide victims.
  • Black Brazilians face disproportionate levels of violence compared to other racial groups.
  • Low-income communities often experience higher rates of both violent crime and police intervention-related deaths.
