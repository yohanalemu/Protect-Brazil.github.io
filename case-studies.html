These case studies provide a deeper understanding of the systemic factors behind crime in Brazil, offering valuable insights into how communities and institutions can work together to address these challenges.

The Bahia State Gang Conflict

Rio de Janeiro has long struggled with the impact of organized crime, particularly in the form of drug trafficking networks. This case examines the favelas, the rise of militia groups, and the efforts by police to curb violence, often leading to controversial outcomes.

Caso Evandro – A Complex Child Murder Case

This 1992 case involves the murder of six-year-old Evandro Ramos Caetano in Guaratuba, Paraná. The investigation uncovered potential connections to political figures and alleged secret sects.

Rio de Janeiro's Police and Militia Corruption

In Rio, a history of police involvement in criminal activities, including partnerships with militias, has perpetuated cycles of violence. These groups often exploit residents through extortion schemes while competing for control over communities.
